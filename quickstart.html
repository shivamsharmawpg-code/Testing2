Welcome! Let’s start birding.

You just tapped the Beak-a-boo NFC sticker. This page is your 5-minute launch guide for heading outside, spotting birds, and logging your discoveries together.

Open the Catalogue Find a Nearby Trail

Before you head out

Bring binoculars, your field journal, and a curious mindset. Keep your volume low, move slowly, and look for movement in trees, shrubs, and along the waterline.

Tip: The best birdwatching happens early morning or just before sunset.

3 quick steps

Step 1: Choose your spot

Pick a park or trail and look for open spaces, quiet paths, and water edges where birds gather.

Step 2: Identify together

Use the catalogue to match colors, sizes, and calls. Tap each bird for fun facts and sound clips.

Step 3: Log your finds

Sketch or write what you see in the journal. Collect species to unlock a digital certificate later.

What to listen for

  • Short, repeating chirps in dense shrubs.
  • Longer whistles from high tree branches.
  • Splashes or calls near ponds and rivers.

Stay curious

  • Swap who holds the binoculars every 5 minutes.
  • Challenge each other to spot three colors.
  • Use the bird whistle to attract nearby songbirds.
